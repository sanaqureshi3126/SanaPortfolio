The Truth About Happiness

Across the world, the strongest predictor of happiness isn’t income—it’s relationships.

The Story Behind the Data

If we live in the most connected time in history—surrounded by people, messages, and constant interaction—why do so many of us still feel lonely? We explored this using data from the World Happiness Report, which shows that people are happier in countries where they feel they have someone they can truly rely on. But that raised a deeper question, because simply being around people doesn't guarantee connection. You can have a full social circle and still feel completely alone. That's where the Harvard Study of Adult Development adds meaning to the data. After following lives for more than 80 years, it found that happiness is not determined by how many relationships we have, but by how well we nurture them over time. Relationships don't stay strong on their own—they require attention, care, and effort. Without that, they slowly fade, and with it, our sense of connection. In the end, happiness isn't about being surrounded by people—it's about having relationships that are strong enough to make you feel not alone.
One of the participants in this 80-year study went on to become President of the United States

function computeMetrics(q) {
  const a = Math.max(0.05, 0.25*q);
  const b = Math.min(1.0, 0.65 + 0.35*q);
  const c = Math.min(1.0, 0.70 + 0.30*q);
  return {
    y: [2.0 + 2.5*a, 3.5 + 4.0*b, 4.5 + 4.5*c],
    risk: [72 - 22*a, 28 - 18*b, 18 - 12*c]
  };
}

const c2 = computeMetrics(1.0);
Plotly.newPlot("chart2", [
  {x:[20], y:[c2.y[0]], marker:{size:[c2.risk[0]], color:"#C8102E"}, name:"Person A"},
  {x:[4],  y:[c2.y[1]], marker:{size:[c2.risk[1]], color:"#2563EB"}, name:"Person B"},
  {x:[18], y:[c2.y[2]], marker:{size:[c2.risk[2]], color:"#1B998B"}, name:"Person C"}
], {/* layout with baseline lines and annotations */});

document.getElementById("qSlider").addEventListener("input", function () {
  const q = parseFloat(this.value);
  const m = computeMetrics(q);
  for (let i = 0; i < 3; i++) {
    Plotly.restyle("chart2", {
      y: [[m.y[i]]],
      marker: [{ size: [m.risk[i]], sizemode: "area", sizeref: 2.0*72/(60**2), sizemin: 12 }]
    }, [i]);
  }
});
